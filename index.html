Abstract

Dust-anchor confinement in strongly coupled dusty plasmas provides a scalable route toward resonant p–¹¹B fusion. In this work we introduce XDUST composites, consisting of BN nanospheres (200 nm) coated with Xe-doped graphene shells (5–10 at.% Xe). Xe incorporation increases electron affinity by 20–30%, stabilizing dust charge and suppressing stochastic losses. As a result, the effective charge increases by ~25% (Z_d → 1.25 Z_d), the Debye length decreases by 15%, and the coherence time extends from 20–30 ns to 50–100 ns.

These improvements enhance resonant confinement (Q increased to 12–15) and raise the fusion yield to (1.8–2.2)×10¹⁸ p–¹¹B → 3α per pulse, representing a 3–4× gain over the BN-only baseline.

Keywords: Xe-doped graphene, quantum catalysis, charge stabilization, coherent fusion, dusty plasma, resonant confinement, p-¹¹B fusion
Core Innovation: XDUST composites introduce quantum catalytic through Xe doping of graphene shells. This addresses the fundamental limitation of charge fluctuations in BN–graphene particles, extending coherence times and enabling higher-Q resonant operation.

1. Introduction: From Static Structures to Wave-Mediated Self-Organization with Quantum Catalysis

Traditional dusty plasma fusion concepts focus on static Coulomb crystallization at Γ > 170. However, this overlooks the rich wave dynamics inherent to dust lattice systems. Dust-acoustic waves, dust lattice waves, and transverse shear modes represent degrees of freedom that can store and coherently transfer energy. When these waves are resonantly excited and phase-synchronized, they enable collective energy focusing impossible in static configurations.

This work introduces a resonant compression paradigm: rather than heating an entire volume, we pre-excite dust lattice oscillations at 1–10 kHz, then apply a precisely timed 50 μs pulse at the moment of maximum coherent compression. This approach addresses three critical challenges simultaneously: (1) reduces energy input requirements by 2–3× through phase coherence; (2) extends hot spot lifetime by using dust particles as electrostatic anchors; (3) enables diagnostic monitoring via resonance frequency measurements that provide direct Γ feedback.

Strongly coupled dusty plasmas provide a unique platform for resonant confinement: collective charge oscillations couple directly to the multi-frequency driving fields.
