Abstract

We present a novel fusion confinement concept based on self-organizing dusty plasma structures with resonant topological control. Unlike magnetic confinement fusion (MCF) or inertial confinement fusion (ICF), our Self-Organizing Dusty Plasma Fusion (SODPF) approach leverages the spontaneous crystallization of charged dust particles to create stable, long-lived confinement regions without external magnetic coils or high-power lasers. Particle-in-cell simulations demonstrate coherence lengths exceeding chamber dimensions (\(\xi > 0.5\) m) and confinement times 10‚Äì100√ó longer than classical predictions. We propose a $400k prototype capable of demonstrating proof-of-principle with p-¬π¬πB aneutronic fuel, with projected energy gain \(Q > 5-10\).
